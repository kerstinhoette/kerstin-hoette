1) Payment data research

Real-time, high-granularity **inter-industry payment data** to measure economic activity, map production networks, and nowcast GDP. I also **host a bimonthly “Payment Data Research” seminar series** — if you’d like to present or attend, please get in touch.

Key outputs

  • GDP nowcasting with large-scale inter-industry payment data — a network approach (with Mantziou, Cucuringu, Reinert). arXiv:2411.02029.
    Introduces GNAR-ex, a network time-series model on UK monthly industry-to-industry payments; improves macro & industry-level nowcasts.
  • Mapping the disaggregated economy in real-time — Economic Systems Research (2025). Article.
    Benchmarks five-digit SIC payment flows against GDP & input–output tables; discusses how to interpret bottom-up payments vis-à-vis national accounts.
  • National accounting from the bottom up using financial transactions data (with Naddeo). arXiv:2407.14776.
    Concept & validation for constructing monthly inter-industry flows from anonymised UK business payments (2015–2023).

4) Technological change & economic development

  • Jee & Hötte (rev. 2025) From catch-up to frontier: the utility model as a learning device to escape the middle-income trap. arXiv
    Shows how utility models seeded domestic capabilities in Korea and became less central as the economy matured.
  • Jee, Hötte, Ring, Burrell (2024) Making IPRs work for climate technology transfer & innovation in developing countries. arXiv
    Finds limited role for patents; highlights trademarks & utility models and tailored policy mixes for mitigation vs. adaptation.

Under review (with short descriptions)

    UM-based learning in Korea increased impact of later frontier patents; effect declines as capabilities mature; fosters domestic specialisation.

    Systematic review + interviews: patents limited; emphasises trademarks, utility models and policy frameworks tailored to mitigation vs. adaptation.
